### 1. Ries & Trout Positioning Strategy

**Core Principle**: "Own a word in the customer's mind"

**How It Works**:
- Identify one specific attribute or benefit
- Become #1 for that attribute in customer perception
- Defend that position consistently

**Framework Structure**:

**Step 1: Competitive Analysis**
- Who are the market leaders?
- What position do they own?
- What's available (not yet claimed)?

**Step 2: Position Selection**
- Choose ONE attribute to own
- Must be relevant to customers
- Must be defensible (you can deliver)
- Must be differentiating (competitors can't easily copy)

**Step 3: Proof Points**
- Product features that support position
- Customer testimonials
- Certifications/awards
- Metrics/data

**Step 4: Communication**
- Every touchpoint reinforces position
- Tagline embodies position
- Visual identity supports position

**Position Ladder** (customer mental hierarchy):

1. **Category Leader** (#1 in category)
2. **Attribute Leader** (#1 for specific benefit)
3. **Niche Leader** (#1 in subsegment)

**Strategy by Position**:
- If #1: Defend and reinforce
- If #2-3: Find unoccupied attribute
- If new: Create new category or niche

### 2. Blue Ocean Strategy

**Core Principle**: Create uncontested market space (Blue Ocean) instead of competing in existing markets (Red Ocean)

**How It Works**:
- Identify factors customers value
- Eliminate/reduce/raise/create factors to differentiate
- Create new demand curve

---

**Application Process**:

**Step 1: Map Current State**
- List all factors competitors compete on
- Rate each factor (low/medium/high investment)
- Identify cost drivers with low customer value

**Step 2: Apply Four Actions**
- **Eliminate**: Costs that don't add value (e.g., fancy packaging for online-only)
- **Reduce**: Over-delivered features (e.g., 100 color options ‚Üí 10 best-sellers)
- **Raise**: Under-delivered essentials (e.g., customer support, quality control)
- **Create**: New value factors (e.g., video tutorials, community, guarantees)

**Step 3: Value Innovation**
- Lower cost structure (from eliminate + reduce)
- Higher customer value (from raise + create)
- Blue Ocean = both simultaneously

---

**Brazilian E-commerce Example**:

**Category**: Suplementos alimentares (supplements)

**Red Ocean** (traditional competitors):
- Compete on: Ingredients, price, promotions, variety
- High cost: Celebrity endorsements, physical stores, massive inventory

**Blue Ocean** (differentiation):

| Action | Factor | Result |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Eliminate** | Physical stores | -50% overhead cost |
| **Reduce** | Product variety | -30% inventory cost |
| **Raise** | Educational content | +300% engagement |
| **Create** | Subscription + coaching | New revenue stream |

**Value Curve**:
- Lower cost: No stores, focused SKUs
- Higher value: Free coaching, personalized plans, community

### 3. Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

**Core Principle**: Customers "hire" products to do a specific job

**How It Works**:
- Understand the functional, emotional, and social jobs customers need done
- Position product as best solution for that job
- Focus on job outcome, not product features

**JTBD Framework**:

**Job Statement Structure**:
```
When I [situation]
I want to [motivation]
So I can [expected outcome]
```

**Example**:
```
When I launch a new product on Mercado Livre
I want to create professional photos quickly
So I can start selling within 24 hours (not waiting weeks for photographer)
```

---

**Three Job Dimensions**:

**1. Functional Job** (practical task)
- What task needs to be done?
- What's the measurable outcome?

**2. Emotional Job** (feelings)
- How does the customer want to feel?
- What anxiety/fear do they want to avoid?

**3. Social Job** (how others perceive them)
- How does the customer want to be perceived?
- What social status do they seek?

---

**Application Template**:

**Step 1: Identify the Job**
- Interview customers: "What were you trying to accomplish?"
- Look for struggling moments: "When did you decide to buy?"
- Focus on progress, not product: "What changed after?"

**Step 2: Map Job Dimensions**

| Dimension | Customer Need | Our Solution |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Functional** | Create 10 product photos | AI generation in 5 min |
| **Emotional** | Feel confident/professional | Pro-quality output |
| **Social** | Be seen as legit seller | Marketplace-ready specs |

**Step 3: Positioning by Job**
- Lead with job outcome (not features)
- Show before/after transformation
- Address all 3 dimensions

### 4. StoryBrand Framework

**Core Principle**: Customer is the hero, brand is the guide

**How It Works**:
- 7-part story structure positions brand as guide helping hero succeed
- Clear, simple messaging that cuts through noise
- Focuses on customer transformation

**The 7 Elements**:

**1. A Character (Hero)**
- WHO: Your customer (not your brand!)
- WANT: What do they desire?
- Example: "Small e-commerce seller wants to grow sales"

**2. Has a Problem**
- EXTERNAL: Visible problem (low sales)
- INTERNAL: Emotional frustration (feels stuck, anxious)
- PHILOSOPHICAL: Why this isn't fair (hard work should = success)

**3. Meets a Guide**
- WHO: Your brand
- EMPATHY: "We understand your struggle"
- AUTHORITY: "We've helped 1000+ sellers like you"

**4. Who Gives Them a Plan**
- PROCESS: Simple 3-step path
- Example: "1. Research product ‚Üí 2. Create brand ‚Üí 3. Launch optimized"

**5. And Calls Them to Action**
- DIRECT: "Start free trial"
- TRANSITIONAL: "Download free guide"

**6. That Helps Them Avoid Failure**
- STAKES: What happens if they don't act?
- Example: "Don't let competitors dominate your niche"

**7. And Ends in Success**
- TRANSFORMATION: How life improves
- Example: "Join 5000+ sellers earning R$ 10k+/month"

---

**StoryBrand Messaging Template**:

```markdown
**HEADLINE** (What customer wants):
[Achieve X outcome] without [pain/obstacle]

**SUBHEADLINE** (The problem):
Most [customers] struggle with [internal problem] when trying to [achieve goal]

**3-STEP PLAN**:
1. [Simple action 1]
2. [Simple action 2]
3. [Simple result]

**CALL TO ACTION**:
[Direct CTA button] or [Transitional CTA link]

**AVOID FAILURE**:
Without [solution], you'll keep [struggling with problem]

**SUCCESS**:
Imagine [transformed state with specific outcome]
```

## üìã POSITIONING CHECKLIST

Before finalizing positioning:

- [ ] **Clear target customer** (specific segment, not "everyone")
- [ ] **Distinct position** (not "we're better", but "we're different")
- [ ] **Proof points** (can we deliver on promise?)

- [ ] **Customer language** (how they describe the problem)
- [ ] **Measurable outcome** (specific transformation)
- [ ] **Brazilian context** (adapted for local market/culture)
