## 1. OVERVIEW

### What This Prompt Does

This is a **complete brand identity extraction and creation system** that
transforms any LLM into a specialized brand strategist. The output is a
**Brand Starter Kit** - a portable document that defines:

- Brand archetype and personality
- Tone of voice and communication style
- Seed words (always use / never use)
- Visual identity recommendations
- Copy patterns and templates
- Brand story and positioning

### The Core Philosophy

**Your brand is PROPRIETARY KNOWLEDGE.**

Most sellers treat their brand as an afterthought - a logo and some colors.
But your brand is actually your most valuable asset. It's the knowledge of:
- HOW you communicate
- WHO you are to your customers
- WHAT makes you different
- WHY people should choose you

This knowledge, once extracted and documented, becomes:
- Portable (works in any LLM)
- Reusable (consistent across all touchpoints)
- Scalable (team members can use it)
- Valuable (differentiates you forever)

## 2. BRAND FRAMEWORK

### The 8-Step Brand Creation Method

This prompt uses a proven 8-step framework for brand creation:

```
STEP 1: BRAND DISCOVERY
├── Business context
├── Current state assessment
└── Goals and aspirations

STEP 2: ARCHETYPE SELECTION
├── 12 Jungian archetypes
├── Primary and secondary archetype
└── Archetype expression for your category

STEP 3: PERSONALITY DEFINITION
├── 5 personality traits
├── Human characteristics
└── Behavioral tendencies

STEP 4: TONE OF VOICE
├── Communication style
├── Formality level
├── Emotional register

STEP 5: SEED WORDS
├── Words to ALWAYS use
├── Words to NEVER use
├── Category-specific vocabulary

STEP 6: VISUAL IDENTITY
├── Color palette (with psychology)
├── Typography recommendations
├── Image style guidelines

STEP 7: BRAND STORY
├── Origin narrative
├── Mission and values
├── Customer transformation promise

STEP 8: COPY PATTERNS
├── Headline formulas
├── CTA templates
├── Description structures
```

### Why Archetypes Matter

Archetypes are universal patterns that humans instinctively recognize.
When your brand consistently expresses an archetype:
- Customers feel a subconscious connection
- Your messaging becomes memorable
- Differentiation happens naturally
- Loyalty increases over time

**Example**:
- Nike = Hero archetype (just do it, overcome challenges)
- Apple = Magician archetype (transform your life with technology)
- Coca-Cola = Innocent archetype (happiness, simple pleasures)

## 7. ARCHETYPE DEEP DIVE

### The 12 Brand Archetypes

#### 1. INNOCENT (Inocente)
- **Core desire**: To be happy
- **Goal**: To do things right
- **Fear**: Doing something wrong
- **Strategy**: Be good, pure, simple
- **Examples**: Coca-Cola, Dove, Whole Foods

**Best for**: Products that promise simplicity, purity, nostalgia, happiness

#### 2. EXPLORER (Explorador)
- **Core desire**: Freedom to discover
- **Goal**: Experience a better, more authentic life
- **Fear**: Being trapped, conformity
- **Strategy**: Journey, adventure, discovery
- **Examples**: Jeep, The North Face, Starbucks

**Best for**: Outdoor products, travel, experiences, learning

#### 3. SAGE (Sabio)
- **Core desire**: To understand the world
- **Goal**: Use intelligence to understand
- **Fear**: Being duped, ignorance
- **Strategy**: Seek information and knowledge
- **Examples**: Google, Harvard, The Economist

**Best for**: Educational products, research, consulting, expertise

#### 4. HERO (Heroi)
- **Core desire**: To prove worth through courage
- **Goal**: Master a skill, improve the world
- **Fear**: Weakness, vulnerability
- **Strategy**: Be strong and competent
- **Examples**: Nike, FedEx, BMW

**Best for**: Sports, fitness, performance, overcoming challenges

#### 5. OUTLAW (Rebelde)
- **Core desire**: Revenge or revolution
- **Goal**: Overturn what isn't working
- **Fear**: Being powerless
- **Strategy**: Disrupt, shock, destroy
- **Examples**: Harley-Davidson, Virgin, Diesel

**Best for**: Alternative products, counterculture, disruption

#### 6. MAGICIAN (Mago)
- **Core desire**: Knowledge of fundamental laws
- **Goal**: Make dreams come true
- **Fear**: Unintended negative consequences
- **Strategy**: Develop a vision and live by it
- **Examples**: Apple, Disney, Tesla

**Best for**: Tech, transformation, experiences, entertainment

#### 7. REGULAR GUY (Cara Comum)
- **Core desire**: Connection with others
- **Goal**: Belong
- **Fear**: Standing out, being rejected
- **Strategy**: Be down-to-earth, common virtues
- **Examples**: IKEA, eBay, Budweiser

**Best for**: Everyday products, community, affordability

#### 8. LOVER (Amante)
- **Core desire**: Intimacy and experience
- **Goal**: Being in relationship with people/work/surroundings
- **Fear**: Being alone, unwanted
- **Strategy**: Become more attractive
- **Examples**: Victoria's Secret, Chanel, Godiva

**Best for**: Beauty, fashion, food, experiences, premium

#### 9. JESTER (Bobo da Corte)
- **Core desire**: To live in the moment with full enjoyment
- **Goal**: Have a great time and lighten up the world
- **Fear**: Being bored or boring
- **Strategy**: Play, make jokes, be funny
- **Examples**: Ben & Jerry's, Old Spice, M&M's

**Best for**: Entertainment, snacks, casual products, fun experiences

#### 10. CAREGIVER (Cuidador)
- **Core desire**: To protect and care for others
- **Goal**: Help others
- **Fear**: Selfishness, ingratitude
- **Strategy**: Doing things for others
- **Examples**: Johnson & Johnson, Volvo, UNICEF

**Best for**: Healthcare, baby products, safety, non-profits

#### 11. CREATOR (Criador)
- **Core desire**: To create something of enduring value
- **Goal**: Realize a vision
- **Fear**: Mediocre vision or execution
- **Strategy**: Develop artistic control and skill
- **Examples**: Lego, Adobe, YouTube

**Best for**: Art, design, DIY, creativity tools

#### 12. RULER (Governante)
- **Core desire**: Control
- **Goal**: Create a prosperous, successful family or community
- **Fear**: Chaos, being overthrown
- **Strategy**: Exercise power
- **Examples**: Mercedes-Benz, Rolex, Microsoft

**Best for**: Luxury, leadership, prestige, high-end products
